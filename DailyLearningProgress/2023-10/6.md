## confer - vi.商议 vt.授予，赋予

解释：

- `"Confer" is a verb that means to have discussions, exchange opinions, or engage in formal conversations, often with the purpose of making decisions or granting something such as an award, title, or honor.`
- "Confer" 是一个动词，意思是进行讨论、交流意见或进行正式谈话，通常是为了做出决策或授予某种奖励、头衔或荣誉等。

用法：

- `confer with sb`   与某人商议
- `confer with sb. on sth.`   与某人商议某事
- `confer a capability`   赋予能力
- `confer by telephone`   通过电话商议
- `confer A on B`   将 A 赋予 B

例句：

- 
  - `The committee will confer tomorrow to decide on the budget allocation for the upcoming project.`
  - （委员会将于明天开会讨论决定下一个项目的预算分配。）
- 
  - `The university will confer an honorary degree upon the renowned scientist for her contributions to the field of physics.`
  - （大学将授予著名科学家荣誉学位，以表彰她在物理领域的贡献。）
- 
  - `The leaders of the two countries will confer to discuss the terms of the trade agreement.`
  - （ 两国领导人将会晤，讨论贸易协议的条款。）

## paradox - n. 矛盾的人（或事物、情况）n. 悖论


解释：

- `"Paradox" is a noun that refers to a statement, situation, or concept that seems contradictory, absurd, or self-defeating, but may actually reveal an underlying truth or complex reality. It often involves a surprising or puzzling twist in logic.`
- "Paradox" 是一个名词，指的是一个看似矛盾、荒谬或自相矛盾的陈述、情况或概念，但实际上可能揭示出深层的真理或复杂的现实。它通常涉及逻辑上的出人意料或令人困惑的转折。


用法：

- `face a paradox`   面对矛盾
- `an apparent paradox`   明显的矛盾
- `a well-known paradox`   为人所熟知的悖论
- `create a paradox`   导致自相矛盾
- `be full of paradoxes`   充满矛盾
- `many levels of paradox`   多重悖论


例句：

- 
  - `The grandfather paradox in time travel theory raises questions about the possibility of changing the past without creating contradictions.`
  - （时间旅行理论中的祖父悖论引发了关于是否可以改变过去而不产生矛盾的问题。）
- 
  - `It's a paradox that the more you learn, the more you realize how much you don't know.`
  - （这是一个悖论，你学得越多，就越意识到自己不知道的有多少。）
- 
  - `The ship of Theseus paradox asks whether a ship that has had all of its parts replaced remains the same ship.`
  - （忒修斯之船悖论探讨了一艘所有零件都被更换的船是否仍然是同一艘船。）

## peculiar - a.奇怪的；特有的，特殊的；不适的


解释：

- `"Peculiar" is an adjective with multiple meanings:`
  1. `It can describe something as strange, unusual, or odd in a way that sets it apart from the norm.`
  2. `It can also mean something that is specific or particular to a person, group, or place, making it distinctive or unique.`
  3. `In legal contexts, it refers to property or rights that are unique to an individual or entity.`
- "Peculiar" 是一个有多重含义的形容词：
  1. 它可以描述某物以一种与常规不同的奇怪、不寻常或古怪的方式。
  2. 它还可以表示某物是特定于个人、团体或地方的，使其具有独特性或独特之处。
  3. 在法律上，它指的是属于个人或实体独特的财产或权利。


用法：


- `a peculiar smell`    奇怪的气味
- `be peculiar to...`    为... 所特有
- `a peculiar institution`   特殊的制度
- `a peculiar feeling`    奇怪的感觉
- `a peculiar idea`    独特的想法
- `feel quite peculiar`    感觉有点不适

例句：

- 
  - `His peculiar behavior at the party raised eyebrows among the guests.`
  - （他在派对上的奇怪行为引起了来宾们的疑虑。）
- 
  - `The town had its own peculiar customs and traditions that were unlike those of neighboring villages.`
  - （这个城镇有其自己独特的风俗和传统，与邻近的村庄不同。）
- 
  - `The legal document outlined the peculiar rights and responsibilities of the landowner.`
  - （法律文件概述了土地所有者的特殊权利和责任。）
